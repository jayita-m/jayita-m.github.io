ABOUT ME

Hello! My name is Jayita and I am a Computer Science student at the University of Texas at Dallas. I have been coding since middle school, when I was first introduced to HTML and CSS.


At UT Dallas, I'm an undergraduate Machine Learning researcher in the Advance Networks Research Lab supervised by Prof. Jason Jue. I work on solving constrained optimization problems using ML and Deep Learning for revenue maximization in 5G networks by distribution of Fog Nodes.


I'm also an officer for the Artificial Intelligence Society where I coordinate and build projects for workshops. I'm also involved in Society of Women Engineers and Association for Computing Machinery.


I have experience in Mobile and Web Development with HTML, CSS and Javascript. I enjoy using frameworks like ReactJS and React Native to build applications that help me in my everyday life. I am trying to work more with other frameworks such as Flutter.


I also have backend development experience with a variety of languages and frameworks. I enjoy using Java, and MySQL for the backend. Firebase is my go-to solution for databases, storage and authentication. I also like using Google Cloud and Azure for my AI solutions.


Outside of school, I really enjoy swimming, uploading videos to my YouTube Channel and being a Taylor Swift fan!

I'm So Hungry

Reverse Recipe Search App

I'm So Hungry is an iOS app that allows users to search for recipes by telling the app what ingredients they already have available in their pantry. This app also encourages COVID-19 lockdown measures by allowing people to make the best use of what they already have at home.

I worked on both the frontend and backend of this app. On the frontend, I coded the screens, components, and the navigation of the app. In the backend, I queried Spoonacular's API using Axios to filter recipes by ingredients.

Garbage Classifier

Convolutional Neural Network Classifier

CNN Garbage Classifier is an ML model to classify images of trash into 6 categories: Plastic, Metal, Glass, Cardboard, Trash and Paper, and hence classify them as recyclable/non-recyclable for better waste management.

I contributed to both data pre-processing as well as modelling stages of this project. This classifier uses 6 Conv2D layers to process the tensors, and predict the texture of image.

Truck monitoring and tracking system

Weather To Wear

Closet organization and outfit recommendation app

Weather To Wear is a full-stack application that suggests weather-appropriate outfits for the day from the user's own inventory. This program is capable of suggesting weather-appropriate outfits for any location, shuffle outfits, and tweet about the user's #OOTD. User can also view their recently uploaded clothing items in an Instagram-like feed.

I worked on both the back-end and the front-end of this application. I utilized Google's Cloud Vision AI to detect logos on branded clothing items, and suggest user to buy more items accordingly. I used Firebase as my backend solution, Weatherbit API for reading the weather, and ReactJS to build the frontend of this interactive app.
